## Color Philosophy

"The Sofka color palette is one of the most unifying elements of our brand identity. We use it to connect our experiences across products and services and all of our digital and print marketing communications. It enlivens our stories without dominating them. It reveals our personality and reflects our diversity and wide range of offerings."

## Typography

- **Primary display:** Clash Grotesk (Regular, Medium, Semibold)
- **Body:** Inter (Regular, Bold)
- "Featuring many fine details, moderate contrast, and slightly unusual anatomy, the typeface can be a loud and proud hero for all sorts of designs."

### Typography Rules

- Minimum contrast ratio: 4.5:1 (text/background)
- Large-scale text (18pt or 14pt bold): minimum 3:1
- Max 3 font sizes or weights per layout
- Sentence case for headlines
- Keep copy flush left
- Min digital height: 20px
- Min print height: 0.22" (5.5mm)

## Logo Rules

- **Minimum height (digital):** 20px
- **Minimum height (print):** 0.22" (5.5mm)
- **Minimum width (digital):** 72px
- **Minimum width (print):** 1" (25.4mm)
- **Clear space:** 1x measurement around logo
- **Co-branding:** 2x distance between logos; partner logo height <= Sofka logo
- **Color version:** Use on light/white backgrounds
- **Inverted version:** Available for dark backgrounds

### Logo Prohibitions

1. Do not place on backgrounds with insufficient contrast
2. Do not place over busy backgrounds
3. Do not compress or stretch
4. Do not add effects (shadows, gradients)
5. Do not alter the symbol
6. Do not add anything to the symbol

## Illustration Style

"Our illustration style may look simple, but it shows a careful understanding of our philosophy. Using simple geometric shapes, a strong grid, and mathematically derived proportions, we can create a wide variety of engaging illustrations. Our Kaizen philosophy inspires abstract illustration and visual metaphors."

### Illustration Rules

- Simple geometric shapes
- Strong mathematical grid
- Mathematically derived proportions
- Abstract illustration
- Visual metaphors inspired by Kaizen

## Grid System

"Without organization, a message might get lost. That's why we must carefully consider the underlying structure of everything we design. Grids might be invisible, but they're essential — for balance, emphasis, and understanding."

## Target Audience

- Businesses seeking digital transformation
- Organizations staying updated in the digital world
- Enterprises needing sustainable and scalable digital solutions
- Companies in Latin America and the United States (primary markets)
